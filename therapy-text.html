Who Should Take Therapy?

Therapy often has a stigma associated with it, given the history of how mental health issues in our society have been addressed. The reality is that we are all struggling with life issues- and anyone can benefit from counseling! It is not just for people who have a rough time or are in crisis. However, it can be stigmatizing to tell someone they should be taking therapy, or they need counseling. So, if you or someone you know are going through unusual thoughts, behavior pattern, feeling such as:...

  • If you’ve been feeling flat, anxious or out of sorts for more days than not for a few weeks
  • If you or others are worried about the changes you’re experiencing
  • If you’re feeling stuck or finding it tricky to shift your thoughts and feelings on your own
  • If you’re observing that your feelings are affecting the way at home, at work or in your relationships
  • If you are having suicidal or self-harm thoughts
  • If you think you need some practical suggestions to help you in coping
  • If you have a history of mental health illness
Learn your mental health status with above-stated situations, accept your condition and recognize that you need therapy, Team HopeQure is there to help you deal with the mental health issues that could be anything; the fear of isolation, unusual mood swings, phobia anxiety and make you realize that you are not alone.

Psychotherapy
Psychotherapy is a concept that encompasses all the talk therapies and the many approaches/methods associated with them. Problems dealt in psychotherapy may vary from mental conditions to emotional difficulties, psychotherapists can use to help you understand and discuss how you feel.

Clinical Therapy
Clinical therapy is specialized in treating and diagnosing mental illness and psychological disorders. Provide clinical treatment including addiction, anxiety, depression, learning difficulties, and relationship issues....


Child Therapy
Child therapy assess and treat infants and adolescents. Child psychologists assess and treat kids and adolescents. They help kids cope with stresses such as parent’s divorce, death, and transitions from family or school. In addition, they deal with children with a variety of developmental problems, ranging from learning disabilities to severe mental illness. A partial list of diagnosed conditions covers attention deficit disorder, ADHD, obsessive- compulsive disorder, phobias, and condition of transition.

Counselling Psychologist
Counseling Psychologists serve persons of all ages and cultural backgrounds in both individual and group settings. Interventions used by Counseling Psychologists may be either brief or long-term; they are often problem-specific and goal-directed.

Marriage and family counselling
Marriage and family counsellors deal with individuals, couples, or families. By handling the relationships of their clients, they discuss mental health problems; help them cope up with problems such as low self-esteem and stress.

Substance abuse Counselling
Substance abuse counselling is a way of treatment to help people handle them and help people break free of their addiction to drugs and alcohol. It helps people discuss and reach out to the root cause of this addiction.

Rehabilitation Counselling
This type of counseling refers to a practice where the counsellor helps people with their emotional and physical disabilities. Furthermore, these counsellors teach these people ways to live independently and maintain gainful employment. In short, they help people in guiding them and assisting them to lead independent lives.

Expressive Art Therapy
Expressive art therapy combines visual arts, movement, drama, music, writing, and other creative processes to help a person express and visualize emotions and facilitate growth and healing.

Duration of therapy

Duration of therapy comprise of three major aspects:

  • Time period of each session
  • Frequency of sessions
  • Total duration of therapy

Difference Between Mental Healthcare Professionals

It can be very difficult to get help with the problems of mind, emotion, relationship or friendship.
Finding the right specialist in mental health will help you to solve every issue in your life. Let us check basics one should know about various types of practitioners in mental health.

Psychiatrist Psychiatrist is a medical doctor specialised in the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of mental illness. A psychiatrist's training starts with a medical degree of four years and includes a one-year internship and a specialist training of at least three years as a psychiatrist. A psychiatrist is trained to distinguish mental health problems from other underlying medical conditions that may have psychiatric symptoms.

Psychotherapist A licensed psychotherapist helps those with stress , anxiety , depression, bipolar disorder, destructive habits of behaviour, depressed emotions and a number of other mental disorders....

Psychologist A psychologist research the mind and behaviour in psychology. They are a graduate in Psychology who can evaluate and treat mental and emotional disorders. Licensed psychologists are eligible for counseling and psychotherapy, psychiatric assessments and mental illness diagnosis. However, they are not psychiatrists. Clinical Psychologists Clinical psychologists assess diagnose and treat individuals experiencing psychological distress and mental illness. They also perform psychotherapy and develop treatment plans.

Counseling Psychologists Counseling psychologists provide psychotherapy to people experiencing psychological disturbances, behavioral problems, emotional difficulties, stress, and related issues. These professionals share many commonalities with clinical psychologists.

Developmental Psychologists Developmental psychologists study lifelong human development. Most of them concentrate on a particular group like early childhood, puberty, adulthood, or old age. These specialists are able to carry out tasks such as assessing children who may be impaired or deficient in their development, researching aging problems, and investigating the learning of language skills.

Organizational Psychologists Organizational psychologist’s studies workplace behavior such as recruiting and selecting employees, choosing best employees for specific jobs and ways to increase worker productivity. They often work alongside with human resource personnel, organizational psychologist utilize his or her knowledge of psychological principles to design assessments to screen candidates for specific job roles. They can also develop training programs for existing employees in order to increase knowledge, maximize efficiency.
